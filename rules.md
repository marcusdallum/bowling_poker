# 🎳 The Bowling Poker Official Rulebook

## 1. The Setup
* **The Deck:** Two full decks of cards, including all Jokers, are shuffled together at the start of every game.
* **Entry Fee:** Each player pays $1 to enter the game.
* **The Pot:** The pot consists of the current game's entry fees plus any amount carried over from previous games/weeks.
* **The Pot Custodian:** If a pot carries over to a new week, the player currently holding the physical cards is the custodian of the money. If the custodian is not bowling the following week, the team must select a player to hold the carryover money.

## 2. Card Acquisition (The Bowling Phase)
Players earn cards based on their performance during their frames (using standard PBA/USBC definitions):
* **Strike (Standard):** 1 card.
* **Strike (Consecutive):** 2 cards (the second strike in a row earns an additional card; subsequent strikes in the same streak also earn 2 cards).
* **Spare:** 1 card.
* **Split-to-Spare:** 2 cards (if a player converts a split into a spare).
* **Non-scoring frames:** 0 cards.

## 3. The Wild Card (The Final Phase)
After the last bowler has finished their turn:
* The winner of the previous round shuffles the cards and selects a player to "cut" the deck.
* The card revealed by the cut becomes the **Universal True Wild Card** for all players in that game.
* **The "True Wild" Power:** The cut card can be used to represent **any rank and any suit** in the deck to create the best possible hand. (e.g., if you have 4 Jacks and a 2, and the cut card is a 2, that 2 can be used as a Jack).
* **The "Natural" Rule:** A hand composed entirely of "Natural" cards (no Wild Card, no Jokers) always beats a hand of the same rank that uses a Wild Card or Joker.
* **The Joker Exception:** If the cut card is a Joker, the deck is re-cut until a non-joker card is revealed.

## 4. Determining the Winner
Winning requires two simultaneous conditions, **unless it is the Season Finale**:
1. **The Poker Hand:** The player with the best poker hand is the "Poker Winner."
    * **The Royal Flush Rule:** A Royal Flush is an automatic win, overriding all other hands.
    * **Tie-Breaker (Poker):** If two players have the same poker hand strength, the player with the higher bowling score for that game wins.
2. **The Bowling Check:** The "Poker Winner" must also have a higher bowling score for that specific game than their opponent(s) to claim the pot.

**The Season Finale Exception:** On the final game of the season, the "Bowling Check" is waived. The winner of the pot is simply the player with the best poker hand.

## 5. The Carryover
* If the player with the best poker hand fails the bowling score check, they do not win the pot.
* The pot **carries over** to the next game.
* The new game's entry fees are added to the existing pot.
* If the pot is not won by the end of the night (3 games), it carries over to the next week.

## 6. The Non-Bowler Rule
Up to two non-bowlers may participate in the game:
* Before the game starts, the group selects the Ace, 2, 3, and 4 cards from the deck.
* The non-bowler picks one of these cards.
* The non-bowler then follows the same card-drawing and winning rules corresponding to the position of the bowler assigned to that card.

## 7. Poker Hand Rankings
Hands are ranked from highest to lowest. In the event of a tie in rank, the **Natural Rule** applies first (Natural beats Wild), then the **Bowling Score** applies.

1.  **Royal Flush** (Automatic Win)
2.  **Five of a Kind**
3.  **Straight Flush**
4.  **Four of a Kind**
5.  **Full House**
6.  **Flush**
7.  **Straight**
8.  **Three of a Kind**
9.  **Two Pair**
10. **One Pair**
11. **High Card**
